We detect the presence of massive-scale emotional contagion via social networks by examining naturally occurring data. Using rainfall as a natural experiment—since rain induces negative affect in those who experience it—we show that people exposed to more positive emotional content in their social feed subsequently post more positive content themselves, and vice versa. Using data from over one billion Facebook users, we find that emotional states can be transferred to others via social network ties, leading people to express similar emotions without direct interaction or awareness. These results demonstrate that the emotional content diffusing through large-scale networks can shape emotional expression on a global scale.
